Why is cybersecurity important?

Good question!

Personal cybersecurity is important for a variety of reasons. In today's society, more and more sensitive information is stored online. Therefore, people are increasingly vulnerable to forms of fraud due to information leaks from data stored on the internet. As cyberattacks grow increasingly complex, using good practices to keep yourself safe online is of utmost priority.

As the visualization on the right shows, this problem becomes increasingly costly as time goes on. Cyberattacks become more prevalent and a larger issue every year.

Types of Cyberattacks

Let's explore two of the most common cyberattacks: phishing and malware.

Phishing

Phishing is a type of cyberattack involving the use of deceptive messages, often by impersonating a company, to lead to websites that trick users into divulging personal information, such as passwords and financial data.

The prevalence of phishing websites has increased in recent years. Use the graph to the right to discover how the number of phishing websites has changed overtime based on which industry sector is targeted.

If you encounter a phishing scam, you should change any compromised passwords, scan your device for malware, and report it to the Federal Trade Commission.


Malware

Let's define malware.

According to Google, malware is "software that is specifically designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system."

Malware can get into your computer through a variety of ways. For example, trojan horse malware can be installed onto your computer by pretending to be legitimate code or hardware.

Best Practices for Cybersecurity

Although cyberattacks are everywhere online, there are several preventative measures you can take to stay safe.


Use secure passwords.

Your password should be unique - use different passwords for different accounts. It should also be long (at least 16 characters!) and contain letters, numbers, and symbols.

Consider using a password manager for organization. Password managers allow you to use long, unique, and random passwords without the need to remember them.


Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) for your accounts.

MFA is a system that requires users to authenticate through two or more verification factors to gain access to an account. For example, you may need to enter your password and approve the request on your phone.

MFA provides additional levels of security and allows you to keep your accounts safe even if your login information is compromised.


Keep all of your software up-to-date.

Software companies continuously update their products to provide better security and protect against hackers. Updating your software is an easy way to ensure you have the best level of security possible.

You can set up automatic updates for your device and apps, and schedule them for times when you're not using your device, rather than updating them manually.


Avoid opening suspicious messages.

Don't open suspicious emails or suspicious messages on other platforms. If an email is from an unusual sender (such as a company you don't have an account with), offers deals that you didn't sign up for, or asks you to download something urgently, it is likely a phishing scam.

Don't click on any links or open any attachments from emails you suspect may be phishing scams.


Use a virtual private network (VPN) to keep your connection private.

VPNs utilize a remote connection between your computer and a remote server to encrypt your data and hide your IP address. VPNs even hide your information from your internet service provider, who can normally monitor your online activity (even in incognito/private mode on your browser).

VPNs are especially important when using public WiFi networks, which do not encrypt your data.
